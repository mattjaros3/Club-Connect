Featured Clubs

Club Archery

Archery welcomes all skill levels. The club serves as an opportunity for students to enjoy their experience of the sport, whether it is to learn for fun or to compete at a national level. Our parent organization is USA Archery, and club equipment can be provided thanks to the USA Archery Collegiate Program Equipment Grant.

Learn More

Club Football

Pitt Club Football is an NCFA (National Club Football Association) collegiate tackle football team that competes against other collegiate club football teams. Pitt Club Football is a registered student organization and club sport at the University of Pittsburgh open to both the undergraduate and graduate student body.

Learn More

Computer Science Club

Pitt CSC exists as tool to build a community of students interested in Computer Science, regardless of declared major. It is intended to be a community of members to develop professionally, engage with each other socially, and explore the field of Computer Science intellectually.

Learn More

Clubs You're Following

Barbell Club

Barbell club is a gym based social club. We host group lifts and other fitness related events.

Urban Gaming Club

The Urban Gaming Club provides opportunities to play games of all types such as card games, board games, and video games.

Music Production Club

The Music Production Club focuses on everything involving electronic music production.

Games-edu

We're the official Pitt student organization for serious and transformational game development! We're dedicated to connecting students and faculty across departments to work on game development projects and teaching students to make their own games!

HR Club

Founded in January 2014 by Alyssa Swanson, Lorraine Grant, Hannah Mayes, and Kelsey Griffith, the establishment of the Human Resources Club (HR Club) paralleled the University of Pittsburghâ€™s creation of the Human Resource Management Major in the 2013-2014 academic year. Its purpose and goal is to expose members to the different facets of the Human Resources Profession and ready them for a career of their own by providing them with networking, learning, and development opportunities. It is important to note that membership is not limited to individuals majoring in Human Resource Management major. The HR Club is open to anyone that sees him or herself as career-minded.

Animal Lovers Club

Pitt Animal Lovers' Club is a service organization dedicated to incorporating Pitt Students' love of animals to campus life. We participate in charity events including blanket drives and dog treat baking sales in order to benefit local shelters and humane societies in Pittsburgh. In addition, we create a network of colleagues all interested in animal-related activities to go out and explore the parts of Pittsburgh that you may not be able to see otherwise! Each year we plan trips to The Pittsburgh Zoo and PPG Aquarium, The National Aviary, and to Rolling Hills Ranch.
